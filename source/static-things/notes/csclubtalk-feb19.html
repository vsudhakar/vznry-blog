UNC CS Club Panel - Tech in Industry

Overview (Who Am I)

I worked at a few different places over the last year or so.

  • HomeWise [Startup]
  • Forbes Middle East [Midsize Publishing Company]
  • Amazon - Alexa Devices [Big Tech]

HomeWise [Startup]

Backend stuff was in Django, frontend client apps were in React Native. I have AWS horror stories here too, maybe not comparable to Bluemix though.

Forbes Middle East [Midsize Publishing Company]

I was all over the place here. I mostly built a machine learning based text summarization / SEO extraction tool. Used TensorFlow and Python extensively for that. On the other side, I wrote a webscraper + load testing tool using Go*. I also did a lot of random cloud management in Heroku for testing, IBM Bluemix** for production.

Also worked for some consulting projects involving AI driven customer service. Wrote chatbot backends using Botmaster to abstract away interaction layers, some NoSQL dbs for state management (oh dear...), and hastily thrown together UI in React. If you are interested in tech consulting oh boy do I have some war stories...

*Go

Yep, never used it before, learned it in a day. Like I said, tech stacks aren't important and do NOT take your formal CS education for granted.

**IBM Bluemix

Holy cow I have some stories.

Amazon - Alexa Devices [Big Tech

Lots and lots of Android programming. There was a ton of cool stuff going on though. Big takeaway: do NOT take your formal database education for granted!
